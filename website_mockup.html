Family Archaeology Fun at Leicester Cathedral
Free outdoor event – hands-on activities for children and families

Cathedral Kids: The Big Dig is a family-friendly archaeology day inspired by the Leicester Cathedral Revealed project and excavations carried out by University of Leicester Archaeological Services (ULAS). Children are invited to dig, discover and play while learning about the stories buried beneath the Cathedral.

About the Event

When: Summer (family sessions during the day)

Where: Leicester Cathedral outdoor space

Who: Families with children and young people of primary and early secondary age

What you can do on the day:

  • Explore sandpit “dig boxes” with replica and 3D-printed finds
  • Wash and handle safe artefacts with help from volunteers
  • Colour and create at a craft station inspired by Cathedral windows and stonework
  • Talk to an archaeologist and ask questions about real discoveries
  • Join simple role-play activities based on life around the Cathedral

The event is designed to be welcoming and accessible, offering a relaxed introduction to archaeology rather than a formal history lesson.

Learn more about the archaeology behind the event by visiting our Leicester Cathedral Anecdotes page, or explore the Cathedral itself via the official website .

Online Activities & At-Home Digging

The online resource extends the event beyond the Cathedral grounds so that families can continue exploring at home.

  • An image-based timeline and anecdotes page showing the Cathedral, the excavations and key discoveries
  • Short, child-friendly explanations of what archaeologists found and why it matters
  • Space for future interactive features, such as a simple “virtual dig” click-and-reveal game
  • Downloadable activity sheets that can be printed and brought to the event for small prizes

These online elements support teachers, group leaders and families who may not be able to attend in person, or who would like extra resources after their visit.

Stories Beneath Our Feet

Excavations linked to Leicester Cathedral Revealed have uncovered graves, walls, floors and thousands of artefacts, revealing how the site has changed from earlier churches and graveyards into the Cathedral visitors see today.

Our Leicester Cathedral Anecdotes page brings together photographs and short stories about:

  • The Cathedral exterior and its changing surroundings
  • How archaeologists opened up and excavated the site with ULAS
  • Objects from everyday life, including pottery, animal bone and other finds
  • Windows, memorials and the reinterment of King Richard III

These stories provide the background for Cathedral Kids: The Big Dig, helping families connect the playful activities in the sandpits with the real archaeology beneath Leicester Cathedral.
